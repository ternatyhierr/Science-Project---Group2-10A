Overview

Solar radiation & EM waves influence climate and ecosystems – but human activities have intensified their effects. UV radiation & greenhouse gases impact biodiversity, agriculture, and human health. Remote sensing helps track pollution, deforestation, and climate changes, allowing governments and scientists to take action.

Impacts Solar Radiation: Climate

Greenhouse Effect And Global Warming
Explore More

The Sun emits solar radiation, which heats the Earth. Some of this heat is trapped by greenhouse gases (CO₂, CH₄) in the atmosphere. Human activities (fossil fuel burning, deforestation) increase greenhouse gases, trapping more heat and causing global warming.

  • Increased
  • Grows Unstable
  • Heat Stress

Impacts EM Waves: Ecosystem

UV Radiation & Ozone Layer Depletion
Explore More

Ultraviolet (UV) radiation from the Sun is mostly blocked by the ozone layer. CFCs (chlorofluorocarbons) and pollution have caused ozone depletion, leading to more UV exposure.

  • Increased Death Of Marine Life
  • Reduced Plant Growth
  • Increased Rates Of Skin Cancer

Remote Sensing In Tracking

Pollution & Deforestation
Explore More

Remote sensing is the process of collecting information about an object, area, or phenomenon without direct contact, typically using satellites, drones, or aircraft-mounted sensors. It helps monitor Earth's surface, atmosphere, and oceans by detecting energy reflected or emitted from different surfaces.

  • Satellites like Sentinel-5P and MODIS detect air pollutants
  • Sentinel-2 tracks forest cover loss & illegal logging.
  • infrared and thermal sensors detect forest fires & heat emissions.
